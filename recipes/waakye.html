Waakye

Description

Waakye is a popular Ghanaian dish that originated in the northern part of the country but has become widely enjoyed across all regions. It is a hearty meal typically served with a variety of accompaniments such as boiled eggs, meats, and spicy sauces. The dish is known for its unique color and flavor, which comes from cooking techniques passed down through generations. Waakye is commonly eaten for breakfast or lunch and is sold by street vendors and in local restaurants, making it a staple in Ghanaian cuisine. It reflects the rich food culture of Ghana and its blend of flavors.

Ingredients

  • Rice
  • Black-eyed peas or beans
  • Dried millet leaves
  • Water
  • Salt

Steps

  1. Rinse the black-eyed peas or beans and boil until slightly tender (about 30 minutes)
  2. Add rinsed millet leaves (optional) to release the color
  3. Add rice to the pot of partially cooked beans and stir.
  4. Pour in enough water to cover the mixture, and add salt to taste.
  5. Cover and simmer on low heat until rice and beans are fully cooked.
  6. Remove from heat once water is absorbed, and the dish is tender.
  7. Serve with preferred accompaniments.
